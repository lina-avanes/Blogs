Blog 6: The Vernam Cipher

The Vernam cipher might just be the only encryption method that has perfect security. It has been mathematically proven to be unbreakable. The Vernam cipher is also knows as the one one-time pad (OTP). One-time pad, since it is only used once, never twice or more.

In order to offer perfect security:
- The key needs to be truly random.
- The key has to be equal to or grater than the plain-text itself

A summary of how it works:
1) Get your plain-text and convert it to binary
2) Generate a key that is totally random , in binary, and it is as long as the plain-text
3) Produce the cipher-text by applying bitwise XOR on the plain-text and the key

Example

Encrypt
  • - First you need to convert the plain-text Hi! In binary

    • Hi! --> 1001000 1101001 0100001

  • - Second you need to generate a random key which is going to be the same length or longer than the plan-text. The key is 0l;@ and it is one alpahbet longer in this case.

    • 0l;@ --> 0110000 1101100 0111011 10000000

  • Third, you need to do the XOR operation on the key

    • XOR for 0l;@ --> 1111000 0000101 0011010 10000000

  • Fourth, you need to convert the XOR result to ASCII to get the cipher-text

    • Cipher-text: X □ □ @


    Decrypt

    In order to decrypt, you need to convert the cipher-text and the key into ASCII and apply the XOR operation.

  • Cipher-text: X □ □ @ --> 1111000 0000101 0011010 10000000
  • 0l;@ --> 1111000 0000101 0011010 10000000
  • Apply XOR to X □ □ @ and 0l;@
  • Result after XOR has been applied: Hi! --> 1001000 1101001 0100001 00000000
  • You will end up with one extra character which is 00000000 or NULL at the end and you can just ignore it. You now have your plain-text.

    • How to make sure the process goes perfectly:
  • This key must be destroyed once used.
  • Assuming it is destroyed and other conditions are met, the Vernam cipher is the only cipher which can offer perfect security.
